Product Information

Based on your medical history, your [Script] dermatologist may prescribe to you a certain treatment. The exact  ingredients are listed on the label affixed to your medication container. Here is some additional information you should know about your prescription.

How do I apply my treatment?

Follow your prescriber’s exact instructions. Rub the product in well and wash your hands after use. Do not share your medications with anyone, especially children. Keep all pharmaceutical drugs away from pets.

What potential side effects should I be aware of?

Side effects are minimal with most topical products. The most common side effects are irritation, reddening of the skin, or a rash at the application site. If any of these occur, please contact your doctor for advice. More serious side effects are uncommon but can occur if the product is over-applied. Report any dizziness, headaches, lightheadedness, or fainting to your SCRIPT doctor immediately, and stop use of the medication. Dosage adjustment or review may be required

How long can I use my treatment?

Compounded prescriptions are labeled with a BUD (Beyond Use Date) that is determined by an expert committee at the United States Pharmacopoeia (USP) and can range from 14 to 180 days based on water activity and how the product is used.

Note that compounded prescriptions are not the same as manufactured drugs, and they do not have ‘expiration dates’. Compounds are individually customized by your prescriber and hand-made to order by specially trained pharmacists and licensed technicians. They are not mass-produced in a manufacturing setting. Due to their custom nature, it is not possible to test every single individually created compound for long periods of time to determine their true shelf life. Thus, a very conservative estimate for BUD is used. These products are likely usable beyond the BUD date given but cannot be guaranteed.

Only your prescriber or pharmacist may offer you advice regarding clinical matters, such as how long to use a product. Keep all products in their original containers and tightly closed when not in use.

Keep all containers clean to prevent microbial contamination.

Store all drugs in a cool, dry, dark place when possible. Heat, humidity, and sunlight reduce shelf life.

Refrigeration can extend shelf life, if appropriate for your medication.

Compounds should be considered as ‘FRESH PRODUCTS’ made to be used immediately, and they should not beheld for later use or stockpiled in advance of needs.

What is tretinoin and how do I use it?

Tretinoin: Tretinoin is a Vitamin A derivative, is one of the most prescribed acne treatments, and is also one of the most well studied. In simple terms, tretinoin speeds up the production of skin cells. This helps the skin to function better by exfoliating toxins and unwanted debris (sebum) that can lead to acne lesions. Tretinoin also increases collagen production and helps impart a healthier, fresher look to the skin.

FEMALE PATIENTS SHOULD NOT USE TRETINOIN PRODUCTS IF PREGNANT. If you are currently pregnant or nursing, discontinue using the product and contact your dermatologist for advice. If you are considering becoming pregnant or actively trying to conceive, contact your dermatologist for advice.

When starting tretinoin therapy, it is important to be aware of some of its expected effects. During the first 2-4 weeks of treatment, you may experience some skin flaking, dryness and some pinkness or mild irritation. This is a normal reaction of your skin to tretinoin therapy as your skin cells speed production. If you experience bothersome, moderate or severe irritation or discomfort, please contact your prescriber for advice.

Tretinoin will also make your skin more sensitive to sunlight, meaning you will sunburn more easily. Avoid using tanning beds or deliberate heavy sun exposure without appropriate protection. Wear sunscreen daily and use hats or scarves as needed to protect yourself. Most prescribers recommend using all tretinoin products at bedtime to help minimize this effect.

‍Niacinamide: Is a perfect partner for tretinoin. Niacinamide has many well-studied effects on the skin and is used internationally as a topical acne treatment. It has anti-inflammatory effects to help with the pain and redness of acne, helps to reduce excessive sebum production, and improves ceramide production.

You must work closely with your Script doctor when you are taking this product. You want to make sure that this medication will not interact with any other medications you are currently taking. Your prescriber may also monitor you to adjust your dosage to be more effective if necessary. If you notice any side effects that you find worrisome, be sure that you contact your doctor as soon as possible. They will advise you as to whether or not you should stop the medication or if dosage adjustment is needed.

What should I know about my custom compounded treatment?

This medicine was compounded specifically for you in a pharmacy to fill the prescription your Script doctor wrote for you. It was specially made to meet your individual needs. For this reason, no standardized information or literature is available with your prescription. If you have not done so, please discuss this medicine with your doctor to ensure that you understand (1) why you have been prescribed a compounded medicine, (2) how to properly take or apply this medicine, and (3) the interactions, if any, this medicine may have with any other medicines you are taking.
